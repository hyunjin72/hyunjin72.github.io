I am a Ph.D student at Machine Learning and Intelligence Lab (MLILAB) at KAIST, under the guidance of Prof. Eunho Yang. My research primarily focuses on the practical deployment of graph-structured data and graph neural networks (GNNs).

My work includes developing data and model pruning techniques to address the computational challenges associated with training large-scale graphs. Additionally, I have focused on enhancing representation learning on text-attributed graphs by employing large language models (LLMs) to transform simplified binary edges into multiple semantic relations. Currently, my primary research interest lies in addressing multimodal complex reasoning by leveraging relational information within a graph-based framework.
